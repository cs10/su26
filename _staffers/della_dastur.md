Della Dastur is a passionate and dedicated Computer Science educator and department chair at Spring Education with more than 10 years of experience teaching programming, computational thinking, and digital design. She is committed to inspiring the next generation of innovators, mentoring students in competitive computer science, and supporting educators through professional development and curriculum leadership.

Della earned a Bachelor of Mathematics with a specialization in Computer Science from the University of Waterloo and a Master of Science in Financial Analysis from the University of San Francisco. Prior to her career in education, she worked as a quantitative analyst at the Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan and as a software engineer at Calypso Technology.

Della also serves as a Teacher Leader for the Beauty and Joy of Computing professional development team. She has presented multiple times at SIGCSE and taught Stanford University's flagship introductory Python course, CS106A.
